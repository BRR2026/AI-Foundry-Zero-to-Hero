## Section A: Multiple Choice — Answers

---

**Question 1 — Answer: B**

> *Azure AI Foundry is a unified platform for building, deploying, and managing AI applications on Azure.*

**Why this matters:** Understanding this definition is foundational. AI Foundry isn't just a chatbot or a code editor — it's a complete platform that brings together models, tools, data, and deployment capabilities in one place. Think of it as a workshop where you have all the tools you need to build AI solutions.

---

**Question 2 — Answer: C**

> *Azure AI Foundry was previously known as Azure AI Studio.*

**Why this matters:** Microsoft rebranded Azure AI Studio to Azure AI Foundry in late 2024. You'll still find many online articles, tutorials, and documentation that reference the old name. Knowing this helps you find relevant resources and understand that they're talking about the same product.

---

**Question 3 — Answer: C**

> *The model catalog contains approximately 1,800+ models.*

**Why this matters:** The sheer number of available models is one of AI Foundry's biggest advantages. Instead of being locked into one provider or one model, you can browse, compare, and choose from thousands of options. This catalog grows regularly as new models are added.

---

**Question 4 — Answer: C**

> *Deploying and testing AI models through an interactive playground is a key capability.*

**Why this matters:** The playground is often where AI development begins. Being able to test a model interactively — without writing any code — dramatically lowers the barrier to entry. You can validate your idea in minutes before investing hours in development.

---

**Question 5 — Answer: B**

> *Azure OpenAI Service provides access specifically to OpenAI models, while AI Foundry is a broader platform integrating models from multiple providers with full application-building tools.*

**Why this matters:** This is a common source of confusion. Think of it this way — Azure OpenAI Service is like a specialty store that sells only one brand. AI Foundry is like a shopping mall that includes that store plus many others, along with food courts, parking, and everything else you need. AI Foundry can use Azure OpenAI Service under the hood, but it offers much more.

---

**Question 6 — Answer: B**

> *The Playground is for interactively testing AI models by sending prompts and observing responses without writing code.*

**Why this matters:** The Playground is your experimentation lab. Before building a full application, you can quickly test different models, system messages, and parameters to find the right configuration. It's one of the most used features in AI Foundry, especially for beginners.

---

**Question 7 — Answer: B**

> *Responsible AI means ensuring AI systems are fair, transparent, safe, and respect privacy, with built-in content filters and safety guardrails.*

**Why this matters:** Microsoft has invested heavily in Responsible AI principles. In AI Foundry, this shows up as default content filters, model evaluation tools, and transparency features like model cards. As an AI developer, understanding Responsible AI isn't optional — it's a core part of building trustworthy AI applications.

---

**Question 8 — Answer: C**

> *The catalog includes chat completion, text completion, embedding, image generation, and many other model types.*

**Why this matters:** Different problems require different types of models. A chatbot needs a chat completion model. A search engine needs embedding models. Knowing what's available helps you pick the right tool for the job instead of trying to force one model to do everything.

---

**Question 9 — Answer: C**

> *The correct URL is https://ai.azure.com.*

**Why this matters:** This is the direct entry point to AI Foundry. While you can also reach some AI features through the general Azure portal (portal.azure.com), the dedicated AI Foundry portal at ai.azure.com provides a streamlined, purpose-built experience for AI development.

---

**Question 10 — Answer: B**

> *An AI agent is an AI system that can autonomously perform tasks, make decisions, and take actions using tools and data sources.*

**Why this matters:** AI agents are one of the hottest topics in AI right now. Unlike a simple chatbot that only responds to questions, an agent can take action — searching databases, calling APIs, writing files, and completing multi-step tasks. AI Foundry provides tools to build, deploy, and manage these agents. We'll explore agents in depth later in the course.

## Section B: True or False — Answers

---

**Question 11 — Answer: False**

> *AI Foundry supports models from many providers including OpenAI, Meta, Mistral, Cohere, and many more — not just Microsoft.*

**Why this matters:** This is one of the most common misconceptions. The multi-provider model catalog is a key differentiator of AI Foundry. You're not locked into any single vendor, which gives you the freedom to choose the best model for your specific use case and budget.

---

**Question 12 — Answer: False**

> *The Playground lets you test models interactively without writing any code. You type prompts and get responses directly in the browser.*

**Why this matters:** The no-code Playground is what makes AI Foundry so beginner-friendly. You don't need to be a programmer to start experimenting with AI. This is by design — Microsoft wants AI development to be accessible to a wider audience, including business analysts, designers, and domain experts.

---

**Question 13 — Answer: True**

> *Temperature ranges from 0 to 1 (or higher in some models). Lower values produce more focused, deterministic responses. Higher values produce more creative, varied responses.*

**Why this matters:** Temperature is one of the most important parameters you'll work with. Understanding it helps you control your AI's behavior. Want consistent, factual answers? Use low temperature. Want creative brainstorming? Use high temperature. Mastering this one parameter will immediately improve your AI applications.

---

**Question 14 — Answer: True**

> *The system message is a set of instructions that defines the model's persona, behavior, and constraints. It's processed before any user messages.*

**Why this matters:** The system message is one of the most powerful tools in prompt engineering. It's the difference between a generic AI response and a tailored, branded experience. In production applications, the system message is where you define your AI assistant's personality, knowledge boundaries, and response format.

---

**Question 15 — Answer: False**

> *Azure AI Foundry is available to anyone with an Azure account, including free tier users. You can explore the portal, browse the model catalog, and create projects without a paid subscription.*

**Why this matters:** Accessibility is important. Microsoft offers free tier options specifically so that students, hobbyists, and small teams can learn and experiment without financial barriers. While some advanced features and higher usage do require paid resources, you can get remarkably far with the free tier.

## Section D: Matching — Answers

**Question 19 — Answers**

| Match | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **A → 2** | Model Catalog = A browsable library of 1,800+ AI models from multiple providers |
| **B → 3** | Playground = An interactive environment for testing AI models with prompts |
| **C → 4** | System Message = Instructions given to the model that define its behavior and personality |
| **D → 1** | Temperature = The setting that controls how creative or random a model's output is |

**Why this matters:** These four concepts are the building blocks you'll use every day in AI Foundry. The model catalog helps you choose *what* to use. The playground helps you *test* it. The system message controls *how* it behaves. And temperature fine-tunes *how creative* it is.

---

**Question 20 — Answers**

| Match | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **A → 2** | Azure AI Foundry = A unified platform for building, deploying, and managing AI applications with models from many providers |
| **B → 1** | Azure OpenAI Service = Provides direct API access specifically to OpenAI's models (GPT, DALL·E, Whisper) |

**Why this matters:** Microsoft's AI portfolio can be confusing because the services overlap. Understanding how they relate to each other helps you choose the right service for the right job:

- Need to **build a full AI application** with multiple models? → **AI Foundry**
- Need **direct API access to GPT** specifically? → **Azure OpenAI Service**
- Need to **train your own custom model** from scratch? → **Azure Machine Learning**
- Need a **quick pre-built AI feature** (like image recognition or speech-to-text)? → **Azure AI Services**
