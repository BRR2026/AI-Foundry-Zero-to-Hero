❌

Azure ML ≠ AI Foundry

Data labeling, feature engineering, hyperparameter tuning, and model training from scratch are Azure ML features — they don't exist in AI Foundry.

⏳

RAG at Week 31 Is Too Late

80% of AI Foundry apps use RAG. Teaching it under "Advanced" means 30 weeks without the platform's most common pattern.

🕳️

Prompt Engineering Is Missing

The #1 skill for AI Foundry users has no dedicated week. Neither do Prompt Flow, function calling, or cost optimization.

🏷️

No Difficulty Levels

All 45 weeks appear at the same level. Learners can't self-assess or skip to their proficiency.

Proposed Curriculum

Three-tier structure: Beginner → Intermediate → Advanced

🟢 Beginner

Weeks 1–15 · 3 Arcs
  • ARC 1: Foundations — What is Foundry, lifecycle, architecture, Responsible AI, setup
  • ARC 2: Prompts & Models — Prompt engineering, model catalog, comparing models, fine-tuning, evaluation
  • ARC 3: Data & RAG — Data sources, AI Search, vector indexes, first RAG app, advanced RAG

🟡 Intermediate

Weeks 16–30 · 3 Arcs
  • ARC 4: AI Agents — Agent framework, tools, memory, function calling, agent evaluation
  • ARC 5: Orchestration — Prompt Flow, multi-model routing, Copilot Studio, M365, Teams
  • ARC 6: Deploy & MLOps — Endpoints, CI/CD, monitoring, scaling, cost optimization

🔴 Advanced

Weeks 31–45 · 3 Arcs
  • ARC 7: Security & Governance — RBAC, Purview, audit trails, content safety at scale, VNet
  • ARC 8: Enterprise — Multi-agent systems, real-time inference, partner offerings, case studies
  • ARC 9: Capstone — Choose project, build, deploy, secure, publish final blog
45 weeks · 9 arcs · 225 deliverables (5 per week) Each week: Guide · Blog · Slides · Lab · Quiz
